## How to Select a Color Theme

1. **Navigate to the Color Theme Section**: It's at the top of the form, right after the header
2. **Click on Your Preferred Color**: Each color is displayed as a circular button with its name
3. **See the Selection**: The selected color will show a checkmark and highlighted border
4. **Preview Your Choice**: Click "Preview PDF" to see how your resume looks with the selected color
5. **Change Anytime**: You can switch colors and preview again before downloading

## Color Selection Tips

### By Industry

- **Corporate/Finance**: Blue, Navy, Slate, Gray
- **Technology**: Blue, Indigo, Cyan, Teal
- **Creative**: Purple, Pink, Orange, Red
- **Healthcare**: Green, Teal, Blue
- **Legal/Government**: Navy, Slate, Gray, Blue
- **Marketing/Sales**: Red, Orange, Purple, Pink
- **Education**: Green, Blue, Teal

### By Career Level

- **Entry Level**: Brighter colors (Cyan, Teal, Green) show energy and enthusiasm
- **Mid-Level**: Balanced colors (Blue, Indigo, Purple) show professionalism with personality
- **Senior/Executive**: Traditional colors (Navy, Slate, Gray) convey authority and experience

### By Personal Brand

- **Conservative**: Navy, Slate, Gray, Blue
- **Modern**: Indigo, Cyan, Teal, Purple
- **Creative**: Purple, Pink, Orange, Red
- **Balanced**: Blue, Green, Teal

## What the Color Affects

The selected color theme is applied to:

1. **Your Name**: The large heading at the top of your resume
2. **Section Headers**: All section titles (Professional Summary, Education, Work Experience, Skills, Projects)
3. **Section Underlines**: The decorative lines under each section header

The color does NOT affect:
- Body text (always black for readability)
- Contact information (gray for subtle appearance)
- Dates and locations (gray for hierarchy)
- Links (blue for web convention)

## Testing Your Color Choice

1. **Fill Sample Data**: Click the "Fill Sample Data" button to populate all fields
2. **Select Different Colors**: Try 2-3 different color themes
3. **Preview Each**: Use "Preview PDF" to see how each color looks
4. **Compare**: Open multiple previews in different tabs to compare
5. **Choose**: Select the color that best represents your personal brand and target industry
